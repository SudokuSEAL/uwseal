Sensors, Energy, and Automation Labratory


SEAL is University research laboratory located in the University of Washington Department of Electrical Engineering. Projects at SEAL are focused on sensor and sensor array design using electric, magnetic, acoustic, and thermal fields. SEAL focuses on developing new products in the area of medical and green energy technologies. The lab performs R&D and P&D by working in collaboration with the NIH, the NSF and other research organizations introducing cutting edge technology to the market..

Key reasons to join SEAL

Get Credits

You can get academic credit while working at SEAL. Most students pick between 1-3 credits depending on the level of commitment they have to the lab.

Experience Research

UW is a world-famous research university and SEAL is a perfect introduction to the world of research. See what UW research has to offer with SEAL.

Skills To Add To Resume

At SEAL you learn by doing. Working on a project in the lab will invariably teach you new skills to add onto your resume.

Possibilities Of Publications And Scholarships

At SEAL we encourage and support students in the quest for publications and scholarships, of which there are plenty opportunities to gain. Publications are excellent for anyone with graduate school on their radar.

Mission Statement

The Sensors, Energy, and Automation Laboratory (SEAL) of the Department of Electrical Engineering is directed by Professor Alexander Mamishev. SEAL conducts research in a variety of areas, including medical device design, consumer products, energy efficiency, security applications, non-destructive testing, and transportation.

THE TEAM

Faculty and Staff

Alexander V. Mamishev

SEAL Director, Professor of EE, Adjunct Professor of ME.

Professor Alexander V. Mamishev received the equivalent of a BSEE degree from Kiev Polytechnic Institute, Ukraine, in 1992 and his MSEE from Texas A&M University in 1994. IN 1999 he complete a Ph.D in electrical engineering and computer science at MIT, with a minor in technology management from Harvard Business School and MIT Sloan School of Management. Currently he is a professor and director of the Sensors, Energy, and Automation Laboratory (SEAL) in the Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Washington, Seattle. Mamishev is author on more than 120 technical publications. He serves as an associate editor for the IEEE transactions on Dielectrics and Electrical Insulation. He is a recipient of the NSF CAREER Award, the IEEE Outstanding Branch Advisor Awards and the UW EE Outstanding Research Advisor Award.

Rania Hussein

Senior Research Scientist, Assistant Teaching Professor

Dr. Rania Hussein is an ECE Associate Teaching Professor, the Founder and Director of the Remote Hub Lab, an Associate Director of the UW IAC, and a Senior Research Scientist at SEAL. Her research focuses on embedded systems, image processing, the Internet of Things (IoT), and engineering education. In addition to her technical expertise, Dr. Hussein has extensive experience in project management and community leadership. She has served as founder, board member, and executive director for non-profits to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion. Before joining UW, she was a Research Engineer at the Walt Disney Company, where she worked on software development and the evaluation of disruptive and cutting edge technologies. To learn more about Dr. Hussein, visit her website.

Sep Makhsous

Electrical Engineering, Ph.D. Program

Makhsous holds a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering and an MBA in Technology Commercialization from the University of Washington. His research interests are biomedical devices, image processing, 3D imaging, machine learning, and the Internet of Things (IoT). Makhsous is currently a postdoc Commercialization Fellow at the CoMotion center in the UW, working under the supervision of Dr. Mamishev and Dr. Novosselov. As a commercialization postdoc, he is responsible for leading a startup called AeroSpec. AeroSpec is an air quality monitoring system that helps the retail segment of the medical systems' landscape (clinics, dental offices, and urgent care). AeroSpec offers real-time particle distribution models to maintain a safe indoor environment for physicians, nurses, and patients, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Aaron Zielinski

Lead Technical Writer

Zielinski is the lead technical writer for SEAL, and a staff member of both the UW ECE and UW IAC. His expertise is in technical communications, public outreach, student integration, and R&D grant proposals. He is responsible for lab reporting, manuscript preparation, and integrating students into SEAL research. He also works with several Seattle-based start-ups to develop tools and systems to optimize task flow.
